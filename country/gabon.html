Gabon officially the Gabonese Republic, is a sovereign state on the west coast of Central Africa. Located on the equator, Gabon is bordered by Equatorial Guinea to the northwest, Cameroon to the north, the Republic of the Congo on the east and south, and the Gulf of Guinea to the west. It has an area of nearly 270,000 square kilometers and its population is estimated at 1.5 million people. Its capital and largest city is Libreville. [R1]

The earliest post office was set up at Libreville in 1862; mail from there was routed through the British post office on Fernando Po (now Bioko, an island hosting the capital city of Equatorial Guinea). Mail used the stamps of the French Colonies general issue, cancelled with a "GAB" inside a lozenge of dots. On 31 July 1886 Gabon issued its first stamps, which were surcharges on the existing stock, to cover shortages of the most-used values. The overprint consisted of "GAB" in dots, plus the new value.
