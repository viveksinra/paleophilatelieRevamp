Overview

The United States has a rich history of paleontological discoveries and has been at the forefront of dinosaur research since the 19th century. From the famous "Bone Wars" between Othniel Charles Marsh and Edward Drinker Cope to modern discoveries at sites like Hell Creek and the Morrison Formation, American paleontology has contributed enormously to our understanding of prehistoric life.

The U.S. Postal Service has commemorated this heritage through various stamp issues, beginning with the 1955 issue honoring the American Association for the Advancement of Science, which featured images related to natural history.

Major paleontological themes on US stamps include dinosaurs, prehistoric mammals, ancient marine life, and human evolution. The 1989 "Prehistoric Animals" series and the 2019 "T. Rex" stamps are particularly notable issues.

Notes

The 1989 "Prehistoric Animals" stamps were controversial because they depicted the Brontosaurus, a name that had been considered invalid since 1903 when the dinosaur was reclassified as Apatosaurus. However, in 2015, research suggested Brontosaurus might be a valid genus after all.

The 2019 T. Rex stamps were issued to coincide with the Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History's renovation of their dinosaur hall.
