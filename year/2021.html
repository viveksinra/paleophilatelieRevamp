Some personalized stamps related to Paleontology: fossils

30.09.2021 Liechtenstein [SP2] xx.xx.2021 Slovenia [SP1] Original Article: The History of Ichthyosaurs Discovery

Notes:

[SP1] This set of self-adhesive, personalized stamps were issued by the Slovenian Geological Society.
Two stamps of the set show photographs of fossils.
In the upper row, far right is a stamp showing Pironaea. Pironaea was a rudist bivalve – rudists were major reef-builders during much of the Mesozoic.
In the center row, far right is an example of a Megalodontid bivalve.
For more details, please go here.

[SP2] Personalized stamp of Liechtenstein, produced by a German philatelic dealer, with real piece of petrified wood on the top-right corner.

 [A3] New stamp from the multi-year set "Beauty and Greatness of Ukraine" was dedicated to the Rivne region and shows a piece of amber from the collection of the Amber Museum in Rivne, Ukraine.
Amber is a fossilized resin of the ancients conifers growing in the area of Rivne region.
The amber is dated between Late Eocene and Early Miocene (about 37-50 million years ago) and suggested to be contemporaneous to Baltic amber.
It seems that the design of the stamp was changed at the last minute, as the stamp depicted in the ”Philately of Ukraine: Magazine” (28/2021) was different.
The original design showed a piece of amber encasing a fossil insect.
For more details click here.

 [A4] On October 12 2021, Hong Kong's Post issued a Mini-Sheet "China World Heritage Full Series".
The Mini-Sheet contain 10 stamps of the natural and cultural treasures.
One of the stamps in the last row show a landscape of the Danxia Mountain.
Very similar stamp, but with different face value was issued in the Souvenir Sheet of Hong Kong in 2014 "World Heritage in China Series No. 3: China Danxia".

Skull fossils of famous Maba Man found in the southwest of Danxia basin in Lion Rock, near the town of Maba, in Guangdong province.
Its age estimated between 200.000 and 300.000 years old and was labelled an intermediate in form between Homo erectus and Homo sapiens.

The Danxia Mountain was inscribed as part of the China Danxia World Heritage Site in 2010 because of its unique geographical in formations and spectacular scenery.
For more details about Danxia Mountain, please click here.

 [A5] The stamp of 120 Fen has the title "Ancient DNA Indicates China’s Prehistoric Human Population Shifts and Admixture" (Genetic Research into Ancestry).

 [A6] Landscape of 4 out of 19 National Parks of Switzerland shown on these stamps – two in the Jura mountains and two in the Alps: the Jura Vaudois Nature Park, the Argovia Jurapark, Parc Ela and Binntal Nature Park
The stamps depict not just pristine nature, but also intact settlements and living traditions. Human presence is tangible: scenery is portrayed as cultural landscapes.

The Argovia Jurapark a wonderfully hilly landscape rich in biodiversity can be found – a green treasure trove.
The park perimeter is formed in the north by the tabular Jura (Tafeljura) and in the south by the last foothills of the Jura chain.
In the Jura rock layers, remarkable fossils appear such as ammonites from the primordial Thetys Sea.
In the sediments, there are also iron-bearing layers, which constitute the richest formation in Switzerland within the Aargau Jura.
Many of these fossils can be seen in Mining Museum in Herznach.


 [A7] These stamps set shows landscapes of three National Geoparks of Vietnam: Đồng Văn Karst Plateau Geopark, Non Nước Cao Bằng Geopark, Đắk Nông Geopark.
All three parks are famous fossil sites of Vietnam.
