About Paleophilatelie


Paleophilatelie.eu is an online resource combining Philately with Paleontology and Paleoanthropology.
This website shows images and provides information about thousands of philatelic items related to Paleontology and Paleoanthropology from almost 200 countries and territories sorted by country and date of issue. The website contains many hundreds of pages describing individual stamp issues and articles related to the “Paleophilatelie” topics, as well as many examples of philatelic exhibits.

If you are visiting the website for the first time, please read the About Paleophilatelie.eu website article to get an overview of the website. The Paleophilatelie website can be explored on all types of electronic devices, including desktop computers, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. However, the website is best viewed on a wide screen, where images, galleries, and text layouts can be displayed more clearly and comfortably.

To keep updated about latest modifications of the website please look at the pages News and Current Year (announcements of new and upcoming issues are here) pages. Visitors are welcome to follow the Author on X” formely “Twitter” or join "Paleophilately" group on Facebook, associated with this website to be able to chat with him and provide their feedback. For the updates about the website development, please click here.

The Paleophilatelie website is administered and owned solely by Mr. Michael Kogan from Germany, a member of the International Association of Philatelic Journalists and several international philatelic clubs. Since 2018 Paleophilatelie.eu has successfully participated in international competitions of philatelic websites in the Electronic-Literature category. This aims to popularize the topic and attract other, especially youth, philatelists.

Dr. Peter Voice of the Department of Geological and Environmental Sciences at Western Michigan University in the USA provides scientific advice and editorial support for the website, since 2019.

Any feedback, comments or even complaints are welcome: admin@paleophilatelie.eu (You can write to the author in English, Deutsch, or Russian).
