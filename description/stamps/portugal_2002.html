On January 1st of 2002 15 European countries replaced their local currency with a common one - EURO (€).
This eased travel for citizens of these countries and made trade between corporations and private persons in the Euro zone easier too. Prices on every item including postage stamps changed to euros as well. The same changes were made for ATM stamps too. Since 01.01.2002, only Euro (€) currency are written on ATM stamps of Portugal,

From November 1999 till March 2002 the Post Authority of Portugal were issued 3-5 sets of vending machine stamps (ATM) per year. "Dinosaurs of Portugal" stamps are one of them. Four different designs of dinosaurs and their footprints, were created by Portuguese artist Jose Projecto, then transformed to stamp-templates (self-adhesive labels) by the graphic-designer Mariana Santos. Fossils of all dinosaurs depicted on the stamps as well as their footprints were discovered in Portugal.

Descriptions of the dinosaurs depicted on these stamps are available in the article about the ATM stamps from 1999.

The ATM dinosaur stamps from 2002 were on sale for two months only. On March 1st, 2002 they were replaced by the labels with EURO currency symbol, therefore the dinosaur stamps with Euro values are very difficult to find.

In 2002 the dinosaur ATM stamps were on sale in four different vending machines: AMIEL Sima 1020, SMD MSPost VE 50 and ePost NEWVISION 2000 and ePost NEWVISION M (Modular).
Three of them printed 7 predefined rates, similar to issues of 1999-2001.
The fourth vending machine - ePost NEWVISION M (Modular), has more than 30 pre-programmed rates for various weight categories. Dinosaur stamps with ePost NEWVISION M values are very rare.
  • AMIEL machines printed text in bold, large font: 19mm width
  • SMD machines printed text in smaller font: 16mm width
  • epost NewVision 2000 machines printed text in smallest font: 13mm width.
The EURO symbol ("€") printed differently by each machine. At least two ink colors were used by the machines: violet and black.
The philatelic desk sold ATM dinosaur stamps with Euro currency of AMIEL and SMD machines only, NewVision machine was not installed at the philatelic desk at that time.

Allosaurus ATM stamp of Portugal with face value of €0.48, printed inverted. The face value corresponds to the 2004 rate of domestic letters with weight up to 100g or letter to Spain with weight up to 20g.


These dinosaur stamps, denominated in Euros, were replaced by "Introduction of Euro coins and banknotes" ATM labels in all vending machines. However, some dinosaur ATM stamps with face values of 2003 and 2004 rates have been observed.
These stamps are not listed in any stamp catalogue.
The origins of these stamps are doubtful.
It can be that the labels with dinosaur design were left in some vending machines after the date they are supposed to be replaced.
Another possibility is manipulation of the service staff, who inserted the old labels to the machine to produce some "rare" and expensive stamps for collectors.
