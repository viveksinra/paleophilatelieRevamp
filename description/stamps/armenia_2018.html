On December 14th, 2018, Post Authority of Armenia - Haypost - issued a set of 2 commemorative stamps, showing some prehistoric animals including a flying reptile Tepejara and the dinosaur Argentinosaurus.
Even though dinosaurs have been found in Armenian rocks, there was not enough material to classify these fossils as any specific species. Due to this, Haypost decided to show classic prehistoric animals like a dinosaur and a pterosaur.

Tapejara on stamp of Armenia 2017, MiNr.: 1092, Scott: 1165.
Argentinosaurus on stamp of Armenia 2017, MiNr.: 1091, Scott: 1164.
Tapejara (from a Tupi word meaning "the old being") is a genus of Brazilian pterosaur from the Cretaceous Period (Santana Formation, dating to about 112 Ma ago). Tapejara crests consisted of a semicircular crest over the snout, and a bony prong which extended back behind the head.

Argentinosaurus (meaning "Argentine lizard") is a genus of titanosaur sauropod dinosaur first discovered by Guillermo Heredia in Argentina. The generic name refers to the country in which it was discovered. The dinosaur lived on the then-island continent of South America somewhere between 97 and 93.5 million years ago, during the Late Cretaceous Period. It is among the largest known dinosaurs.
